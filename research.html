Research

Research focus

I use Empirical Software Engineering techniques to identify the strengths and shortcomings in the quality of software requirements models. The main goals of my research can be summarized as follows:

  1. Evaluate the quality of requirements models in terms of appropriateness recognizability (understandability), and learnability
  2. Propose solutions for the improvement of requirements models' quality
  3. Empower developers and other stakeholders to more effectively understand and review requirements models

Research center

My home is the Software Systems group of NOVA LINCS, Faculdade de Ciências e Tecnologia of Universidade NOVA de Lisboa, where I'm advised by Miguel Goulão and João Araújo. The Software Systems group has different teams, and I'm part of the Automated Software Engineering team.

In 2017, I was a Visiting Research Student at SEGAL Research Group (Software Engineering Global interAction Lab), University of Victoria (Canada), working with Daniela Damian.
